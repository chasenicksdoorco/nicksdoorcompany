# Nick's Door Company — Sales Scripts for PM Prospecting

---

## 📞 COLD CALL SCRIPT

**[Getting past the front desk]**

> "Hi, this is [Your Name] with Nick's Door Company. I'm looking to speak with whoever handles your dock door and loading equipment maintenance — would that be your Facilities Manager or Maintenance Director?"

**[If they ask what it's about]**

> "We specialize in commercial dock equipment — dock levelers, truck restraints, overhead doors — and I wanted to see if you folks have a preventive maintenance program in place for your loading docks. Just takes a couple minutes."

---

**[Once you reach the right person]**

> "Hey [Name], this is [Your Name] with Nick's Door Company out of Taylorsville. We've been servicing commercial dock equipment across the Salt Lake Valley since 1995 — dock levelers, truck restraints, sectional doors, high-speed doors, the whole nine.

> I'm reaching out because we're expanding our preventive maintenance program and I know you guys run a big operation over there with a lot of dock doors. 

> Quick question — do you currently have a PM contract in place for your dock equipment, or is it more of a break-fix situation right now?"

**[If they have a PM provider]**

> "Totally understand. How's that been working out for you? A lot of places we talk to are frustrated with response times or find their current provider misses things. I'd love to just swing by, take a quick look at your equipment, and give you a no-obligation comparison. Worst case, you get a second set of eyes on everything."

**[If they DON'T have a PM program]**

> "That's actually pretty common, and honestly that's where we help the most. What usually happens is a dock leveler or a door goes down during a busy shift and now you've got trucks backed up and product sitting. Our PM program catches those problems before they turn into emergencies. 

> What I'd like to do is stop by for 20 minutes, walk your docks, and put together a quick assessment — no charge, no pressure. I can usually spot the stuff that's about to fail before it does. When's a good time this week?"

**[Closing]**

> "Great. I'll plan on [day/time]. I'll bring some info on our PM plans so you can see what's included. What's the best number to reach you at directly in case anything changes?"

## 💡 TIPS

- **Best time to call:** Tuesday-Thursday, 8-10 AM (before their day gets crazy)
- **Don't sell on the first call** — just get the site visit scheduled
- **Bring business cards + a branded PM brochure** to leave behind
- **Take photos** during the walk-through (with permission) — show them the problems you find
- **Follow up within 48 hours** of the site visit with a quote
- **The magic phrase:** "I can usually spot what's about to fail before it does" — that's what gets you in the door
- **For food facilities:** Mention FDA/USDA compliance — dock door seals and functionality are part of their food safety audits
